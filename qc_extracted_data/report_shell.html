Quantpedia × QuantConnect — Reproducibility Audit

The Ledger

800 published trading strategies, each independently re-run by QuantConnect from 2000 to today. This audit splits every equity curve at the exact year the original academic paper's data stopped, isolating the years the paper never saw — and measures what actually happened in them.

How the split works — the "Backtest period from source paper" field (e.g. "1961–2018") states the years of data the original paper used. QuantConnect's re-implementation always simulates from 2000-01-01 forward, so this ledger cuts each strategy's daily equity curve at January 1 of (paper end year + 1). Everything after that date is data the paper's authors never had — a genuine out-of-sample test. Years before the cut overlap the paper's own sample and are shown only as context, never as validation. Strategies whose paper period runs up to (or past) the last available backtest date have no OOS window and are marked "insufficient data" rather than scored.
